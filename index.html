MISSION

To foster the personal and professional development of ambitious Black and Latino college students through direct and intense coaching

PROGRAM

The “Fellowship of Merit” is a 13-week program for Black and Latino college students pursuing careers in financial services.

FoM will build students to be the best version of themselves as people and professionals. Students can expect to learn foundational skills to prepare them to be excellent candidates.

Deadline to apply is Sunday, August 27 at 11:59pm.

REQUIREMENTS

  • Identify as Black/African-American or Hispanic/Latino
  • Attending an accredited college in the New York City metro area (NJ/CT included)
  • Majoring in Accounting, Economics, Finance, or another financial services-related topic
  • Cumulative GPA of 3.3 (sophomore), 3.5 (junior), 3.7 (senior); freshmen do not have a required minimum GPA

FAQ

What will I get once I graduate from the program?

Each graduate will receive a $250 stipend, a volunteer mentor, a custom plaque, a professional LinkedIn headshot, letter of recommendation, and become an official RED Leader.

Additionally, one student will be awarded the “Leader of the Cycle”. The recipient of this award receives a Training The Street bundle ($500 value), an additional custom plaque, and recognition on all social media platforms.

What is the time commitment?

The program will be 13 weeks long. You will attend in-person/virtual workshops on Tuesdays and Thursdays between 6pm and 8:30pm. There will be a total of 26 workshops (20 in-person, 6 virtual).

The first workshop is September 5th. The final workshop is December 7th.

Where will the workshops be hosted?

In-person workshops will be hosted in Manhattan, with access to all major transit lines.

Virtual workshops will be hosted via Zoom.

How is this program different from any other program?

Our culture will differ from any organization you have heard of. Our program will instill discipline, reward on merit, and more importantly, provide you with valuable and accurate career information.

Who will teach the workshops?

The founder, Nick Guillen, will conduct all of the workshops.

Will I receive a mentor?

Upon successful completion of the 13-week program, you will receive a mentor working in the financial services industry.

I am a graduate student. Am I eligible?

No.

If I am not Black or Latino, am I eligible for this program?

No, you must identify as Black or Latino.
